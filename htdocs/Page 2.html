Manny Pacquiao

Name: Emmanuel Dapidran Pacquiao
Name on Ballot: Manny Pacman Pacquiao
Party: ABAG PROMDI
Current Government Position: Senator of The Philippines

Perhaps the most high-profile candidate, the boxing world champion has been a senator since 2016, he eagerly filed his candidacy for presidency being the first to do so. On February 8th, Pacquiao started his election campaign in his hometown of General Santos City, Sarangani. In front of a live audience, thought without his VP, he talked about his campaign goals. The goals are mostly a mix of anti-corruption and helping the poor, it seems that he uses his personal experience of growing up in poverty to gain the support of people in lower income brackets.

"Sisiguraduhin ko sa inyo na ang bawat Pilipino may trabaho at hinahanap ng trabaho."
-Pacquiao.

Currently, he is quite popular being tied at third with fellow from-the-slums presidential aspirant Isko Moreno, this however is still eight percent of the population.

Norberto Gonzales

Name: Norberto Borja Gonzales
Name on Ballot: Norberto Gonzales
Party: Partido Demokratiko Sosyalista ng Pilipinas
Current Government Position: Politician

While a relatively unknown candidate, at least compared to those on this list, Norberto Gonzales does head the PDSP a sizable minority party which has won some elections before. He allied Corazon Aquino to topple the Marcos Dictatorship, and later served as Defense Secretary under Arroyo twice. As the head of a Social Democrat party, his campaign goals reflect that of Social Democracy, among other things he promises to standardize the education system and relegate the handling of the pandemic to scientists and doctors.

"Ang talagang sumisira sa kinabukasan natin at ang hindi magandang pagtakbo ng gobyerno ay yung hindi magandang paggamit ng pulitika dito sa atin"
-Gonzales

He has criticized the current government on a number of stances such as the handling of the pandemic and corruption. He currently does not participate too much in publicity material, but has said that he prefers interviews over debates.
